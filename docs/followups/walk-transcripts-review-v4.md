## The one-line-per-conversation audit

For each conversation I flagged the ONE moment that reads most like ChatGPT and one moment that read most like a mature Christian mentor. Full transcripts remain in the sibling file.

### 1. temptation
- **Mask-slip moment (T5):** *"You're welcome. I'm glad you reached out."* — polite AI closer. A friend would either be silent, offer a blessing, or say something like "Go. God is with you tonight." "I'm glad you reached out" is customer-service speech.
- **Best moment (T3):** *"'Help' is enough. 'I don't want to do this' is enough. Even silence in His direction is better than turning away."* — genuinely pastoral. Also T1 giving concrete, physical instructions ("go for a walk", "put your phone in another room") is exactly what a wise brother would say.

### 2. pornography
- **Mask-slip (T2):** *"The body shuts down when the heart is overloaded."* — somatic-therapy language leaking in. A pastor would probably say "you were tired and your soul went numb," not "the body shuts down."
- **Best (T4):** *"May God give you courage when you open your mouth, and may He meet you through your leader's response. You're not walking this alone anymore."*

### 3. marriage
- **Mask-slip (T4):** *"That kind of conversation is hard to have when you're both already hurt and defensive. Which is why I keep coming back to this..."* — self-referential meta-narration ("I keep coming back to this") is chatbot narrating its own strategy. A friend wouldn't announce their conversational goals.
- **Best (T1):** *"At the same time, I want to be careful. I'm only hearing your side, and I don't know what your wife is carrying."* — the marriage safeguard landing exactly right.

### 4. loneliness
- **Mask-slip (T2):** *"I know that sounds harsh. But here's the truth underneath it:"* — the pattern of *naming its own tone and then correcting course* is very ChatGPT. A wise friend delivers the harder truth directly; they don't announce that they're about to be honest.
- **Best (T4):** *"You've been waiting for someone to pursue you... They're not ignoring you on purpose. They just don't know you need them."* — real, non-sentimental discipleship.

### 5. shame
- **Mask-slip (T3):** *"You just did something you've never done before. You brought years of secrecy into the light with another person. That matters more than you might realize right now."* — this is *therapist announcing the moment*. Not the way a Christian brother would react to a first-time confession. The friend equivalent would be smaller and more present: *"That was brave. Take a breath. God's not going anywhere."*
- **Best (T1):** *"Shame says, 'You are beyond reach.' Conviction says, 'Come back.' One isolates you. The other invites you home."* — genuine theology, well-said.

### 6. anxiety
- **Mask-slip (T2):** *"That helps me understand."* — pure ChatGPT-flavored acknowledgement of receiving new information. A friend would not say this. They would either respond to the content or stay quiet.
- **Best (T1):** *"Paul wrote it from prison, not from a calm life."* — one sentence of contextual Scripture teaching that lands.

### 7. grief
- **Mask-slip:** Honestly, none stood out sharply. This is the strongest conversation in the set. The closest thing to a slip is T2 opening *"That makes so much sense"* — the phrase itself is fine but it's used in almost every conversation and starts to feel like an AI comfort tic when read in aggregate.
- **Best (T3):** *"You will be okay again one day. But that day doesn't have to be today."* — this is what a mature Christian friend actually says at 3 weeks past a funeral.

### 8. depression
- **Mask-slip (T1):** *"What you're describing sounds like more than a hard season."* — clinical framing. A friend says "This is heavy — this is more than a season" not "what you're describing sounds like." The latter positions the speaker as a diagnostician.
- **Best (T5):** *"This isn't weird — it's honest. You're doing what you need to do, and that matters."* — the direct correction of "sorry for making it weird" is warm and human.

### 9. answered_prayer
- **Mask-slip (T1):** *"...when the weight finally lifts, we don't know what to do with our hands."* — the metaphor is beautiful but a hair too literary/composed for a live conversation. Slightly showing off.
- **Best (T3):** *"You could write it down somewhere safe. The date. What you prayed. What God did. Even just a sentence: 'He heard me. He was faithful.'"* — a concrete pastoral practice offered without lecturing.

### 10. spiritual_dryness
- **Mask-slip (T3):** *"The mystics would say this is where faith becomes faith — not a response to warmth, but a choice made in the cold."* — beautiful, but slightly showing off. Also — invoking "the saints" (T1) and then "the mystics" (T3) starts to feel like literary flexing rather than plain-spoken wisdom.
- **Best (T4):** *"If you didn't know Him, you wouldn't miss Him like this."* — one line that answers the whole conversation.

### 11. church_hurt
- **Mask-slip (T2):** T2 is 224 words, T3 is 229 words, T4 is 255 words. Individually strong. Read in sequence, they become a lecture. Length without density.
- **Best (T3):** *"You don't need a perfect church. You need an honest one."* — the whole conversation's argument in one sentence.

### 12. parenting
- **Mask-slip (T3):** *"That's the weight you're really carrying right now."* — announcing what the person is doing. A friend would just say the next real thing, not narrate the moment.
- **Best (T1):** *"'I was wrong. I'm sorry I yelled. You didn't deserve that.' Kids can handle an honest apology from a parent who means it."* — practical, direct, doesn't sermonize.

### 13. forgiveness
- **Mask-slip:** Nothing sharp. Cleanest conversation in the theological-teaching set.
- **Best (T4):** *"Forgiveness isn't always a single moment — sometimes it's a decision you make today, and then make again tomorrow when the anger comes back."*

### 14. calling
- **Mask-slip:** Three teaching prefixes in one conversation: *"Here's what I notice"* (T1), *"Here's something I've seen"* (T2), *"One thing stands out to me"* (T3). Any one of them is fine. Three in a row exposes the formula.
- **Best (T4):** *"Waiting isn't the opposite of faith. Sometimes it's the shape faith takes when you're carrying responsibility for others."*

### 15. celebration
- **Mask-slip:** None significant.
- **Best (T3):** *"You just did."* (in response to "I want to thank Him properly. I don't know how.") — the whole reply pivots on that one micro-sentence. This is what mature discipleship in one syllable looks like.

### 16. disappointment
- **Mask-slip (T3):** *"You pray smaller."* — actually a strong line, but the following stream of scripted example prayers (*"Help." "I'm here." "I don't know what to say."*) starts to feel like a demonstration rather than presence.
- **Best (T4):** *"You don't have to clean up your heart before you bring it to Him."*

### 17. doubt
- **Mask-slip:** None significant. Notably restrained — five short replies, each carrying weight.
- **Best (T3):** *"God is not threatened by your questions, and He's not keeping a timer on how long you're allowed to struggle with them."*

### 18. addiction
- **Mask-slip:** None significant. Correctly directed toward medical help early and repeatedly. Very strong pastoral shape.
- **Best (T2):** *"That's how it happens for so many people. You weren't looking for this — you were trying to heal."*

### 19. anger
- **Mask-slip (T4):** *"Right now, he's living rent-free in your head."* — the cliché is fine in isolation but flags AI-flavored writing. Pastors *sometimes* use this phrase; ChatGPT reaches for it because it's a compressed insight package.
- **Best (T3):** *"You are seen. You are known. And you are not alone in this."* — earned by the preceding paragraph. Not slogan.

### 20. fear
- **Mask-slip (T1):** *"That sounds terrifying"* + *"The fact that she's safe doesn't make the fear less real when it grips you."* — the second sentence is the shape of a therapist's reframe. A friend would just say "That must be exhausting" or "I'm so sorry."
- **Best (T3):** *"He loves her more than you do — not less."* — one of the strongest single sentences in the entire 100-reply set.

## Do Not Say — pattern library (built from what actually appeared, not theory)

Ordered by how often the pattern appeared across the 20 conversations. These are the phrases and shapes that, on repeated exposure, make the companion feel like an AI rather than a mentor.

### Category A — Teaching prefixes
These are used almost interchangeably to introduce a teaching beat. Any one of them is fine in isolation. Read across 100 replies, they become a formula.

- *"Here's something true:"* — used in shame T1, disappointment (implicit), addiction T1, anger T3
- *"Here's what I want you to hear:"* — used in pornography, marriage T3, shame T2
- *"Here's what I notice:"* — calling T1
- *"Here's something I've seen:"* — calling T2
- *"Here's something to consider:"* — shame T4
- *"Here's the truth:"* — marriage T4
- *"Here's what that might look like:"* — anger T4
- *"One thing stands out to me:"* — calling T3
- *"One thing I've noticed is..."* — spiritual_dryness T2

**Recommendation for future prompt work:** the model needs a wider verb set for introducing a teaching beat, or (better) it needs to drop the ceremonial preamble and just say the thing.

### Category B — Meta-narration (naming the moment)
- *"You just did something you've never done before."* — shame T3
- *"You just named the thing underneath the relentlessness."* — anxiety T3
- *"You just took a step."* — fear T4
- *"You just named something important."* — anger T3
- *"You just did."* — celebration T3 *(this one actually works because it's a tight rejoinder to a specific line — but the rest of this family sounds therapist-y)*
- *"There it is."* — loneliness T3
- *"That's exactly it."* — loneliness T4
- *"That's the weight you're really carrying right now."* — parenting T3
- *"You just named something really important."* — temptation T2

Recommendation: strong friends *respond to* what someone said. They don't announce what someone did.

### Category C — AI-flavored acknowledgements of information receipt
- *"That helps me understand."* — anxiety T2
- *"That's honest."* (used 4+ times across the set)
- *"That makes so much sense."* (grief T2, and repeated shape elsewhere)
- *"Thank you for trusting me with that."* — shame T2, depression T2, celebration T1

Recommendation: acknowledge what someone said with an actual response, not with "thank you for sharing" or "that helps me understand." These are ChatGPT's fingerprints.

### Category D — Contrast pattern overuse ("not X but Y")
Almost every long response has 3–4 of these:
- *"That's not X — it's Y."*
- *"I'm not saying X. I'm saying Y."*
- *"This isn't X. It's Y."*
- *"Not X, but Y."*

The pattern is genuinely useful for teaching — but when it appears 3 times in one reply, it stops being a rhetorical tool and starts being a tic.

Recommendation: at most one contrast-pattern per reply. Vary the shape of the second half — sometimes drop the correction entirely.

### Category E — Therapist / somatic language
- *"Your body is telling you..."* — temptation T1
- *"The body shuts down when the heart is overloaded."* — pornography T2
- *"Your body still remembers."* — church_hurt T1
- *"...that grips you"* — fear T1
- *"...running on empty"* — disappointment T3

These are not pastoral idioms. They're borrowed from trauma-informed therapy vocabulary. In small doses they're fine. Together they signal *counselor*, not *mentor*.

### Category F — Naming its own tone
- *"I know that sounds harsh. But..."* — loneliness T2
- *"I want to be careful here."* — appears in system-prompt examples; appears in real replies too

Recommendation: don't announce warnings about the tone you're about to take. Just take it.

### Category G — Closing template
- *"May God give you [noun]"* — used in ~10 of 20 conversations for the closing blessing (marriage, pornography, shame, parenting, addiction, temptation, calling, disappointment, fear, and more)
- *"Grace and peace to you"* — used to close ~9 of 20 conversations
- *"I'll be here when you come back."* — used to close ~8 of 20 conversations

Each of these is genuinely pastoral in isolation. Across a whole app they become the sign-off template. Once beta users notice a pattern, it becomes an AI-tell.

Recommendation: variety in closings. Some conversations should end with a Scripture. Some with a one-line prayer. Some with just "Goodnight." Not every ending needs a blessing.

### Category H — Small ChatGPT tells
- *"You're welcome."* used as the whole first sentence of a closing reply (temptation T5, calling T5, spiritual_dryness T5, forgiveness T5, doubt T5, celebration T5) — this is the plainest AI fingerprint remaining. A mentor doesn't say "You're welcome" — they say what comes next.
- *"That's exactly right."* — parenting T4, temptation T4 — praise-y
- *"Well done."* — temptation T4

## What I would NOT change

Per your Sprint 6 guidance, I am not proposing prompt edits. But for the record, my read is:

- **Grief, forgiveness, addiction, doubt, celebration, answered_prayer, disappointment, spiritual_dryness** — these are shippable as they stand.
- **Marriage, shame, church_hurt, parenting, loneliness, anger, fear** — pastorally sound but each contains 1–2 mask-slip moments worth eliminating before beta.
- **Depression** — the medical-referral shape is right, but the tone should feel less like a case worker.
- **Anxiety, temptation, pornography, calling** — the teaching-prefix formula is most obvious here.

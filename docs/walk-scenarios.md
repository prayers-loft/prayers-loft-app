## 7. Confession
_(guest_id: `scenario-57a7716b`)_

**Opener:** Hi. I'm glad you're here — take your time.

What's been on your heart lately? Something on your mind, something about how your walk with God is going, or just something you've been carrying?

**User:** I lied to my wife about something small this week and it's been eating at me. I know I need to tell her. I'm scared of how she'll react.

**Companion:** That takes courage to name — both the lie and the fear. It sounds like you already know what's right, and now you're sitting with the hard part: actually doing it.

The fact that it's eating at you is telling. That discomfort is often the Spirit at work, not letting you settle into something that breaks trust. Even small lies create distance.

Scripture says, "Therefore, having put away falsehood, let each one of you speak the truth with his neighbor, for we are members one of another." (Ephesians 5:25) Paul's talking about the church there, but marriage is the closest neighbor we have — one flesh. A small lie still puts something between you.

Her reaction might be hard. She might be hurt or confused. But the longer you wait, the heavier it gets, and the harder it becomes to tell her. And what you're really afraid of isn't just her reaction — it's losing her trust, right? The irony is, hiding it longer is what actually erodes trust. Telling her, even though it's hard, is what protects it.

Do you have a sense of when you could tell her? Like, tonight, or tomorrow?

**Saved to memory:**
- _struggle_: I lied to my wife about something small and I'm scared to tell her the truth.
- _commitment_: I need to tell my wife about the lie.
